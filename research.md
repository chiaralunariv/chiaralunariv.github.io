## Research

My research sits at the intersection of sign language linguistics, cognitive neuroscience, and psycholinguistics. Below are the main threads of my current and upcoming work.

### Sign language processing & motion capture
Development of pipelines to quantify sign language production using motion tracking (MediaPipe, OpenPose), including measures of bimanual hand coordination (location symmetry and speed synchronization) across languages such as LSE and RSL.

### Rhythm and timing cognition
As part of the ERC-funded HUMAN RHYTHM project (Rhythm & Brains Lab, UCLouvain / RITMO, University of Oslo), I study rhythm perception and production, extending this work to Deaf and Hard-of-Hearing participants as a novel population for the field.

### Reading and eye-tracking
Eye-tracking studies (invisible boundary paradigm) comparing Deaf/Hard-of-Hearing and hearing readers, examining phonological and orthographic priming and how proficiency (LexTALE) modulates these effects.

### Methods
MEG/EEG, RSA (representational similarity analysis), linear mixed-effects modeling (`lme4`/`lmerTest`), frequency-domain (FFT) analysis, and motion capture pipelines in R.
